Project Overview

The Challenge

Peggy Reid Ogden, an established contemporary artist, needed a digital presence that would properly showcase her artwork while appealing to both casual art enthusiasts and serious collectors. The existing website failed to capture the emotional impact of her pieces and lacked the functionality needed for art sales.

The Solution

I designed a gallery-focused experience that prioritizes artwork presentation while incorporating subtle UX patterns that guide visitors through an emotional journey, from discovery to potential acquisition.

250% Increase in inquiry emails
85% Longer gallery sessions
12 Sales in first 3 months

Understanding the Art World

Art Collector Research

I interviewed 8 art collectors and gallery visitors to understand how they discover, evaluate, and purchase artwork online. The findings revealed unique behaviors that differ significantly from typical e-commerce patterns.

Emotional Connection First

Collectors make emotional decisions first, then rationalize with technique, provenance, and price

High-Quality Imagery Critical

Image quality can make or break a sale - collectors need to see texture, color accuracy, and scale

Artist Story Matters

The artist's background, process, and inspiration significantly influence purchasing decisions

Gallery Experience Analysis

I visited 12 galleries and analyzed 25 artist websites to understand how successful art presentation works both physically and digitally.

Physical Gallery Insights:
  • Minimal distractions allow focus on artwork
  • Proper lighting and spacing create contemplative atmosphere
  • Information is available but not intrusive
  • Natural viewing flow from piece to piece
Digital Opportunities:
  • Zoom functionality for detail viewing
  • Multiple lighting scenarios
  • Contextual room mockups
  • Artist process videos

Audience & Gallery Experience Goals

Target Visitors

The website needed to serve both experienced art collectors and newcomers to art collecting, each with different needs and comfort levels when viewing and purchasing artwork online.

Experienced Collectors

  • Seeking high-quality artwork presentation
  • Want to assess technique and authenticity
  • Interested in artist background and process
  • Need scale and context references

New Art Enthusiasts

  • Looking for pieces that resonate emotionally
  • Want to understand the artist's story
  • Need confidence in their purchasing decisions
  • Prefer accessible, non-intimidating presentation

Technical Considerations

Image Optimization

Implemented progressive image loading and multiple resolution serving to ensure high-quality artwork display without sacrificing performance.

  • WebP format with JPEG fallbacks
  • Responsive image sizing
  • Lazy loading for gallery pages
  • Color-accurate display profiles

Mobile-First Gallery

Designed touch-friendly interactions and optimized layouts for art viewing on all screen sizes.

  • Swipe gestures for gallery navigation
  • Pinch-to-zoom functionality
  • Optimized touch targets
  • Landscape/portrait adaptations

Performance Optimization

Ensured fast loading times while maintaining image quality critical for art appreciation.

  • Critical CSS inlining
  • Image preloading strategies
  • Minimal JavaScript footprint
  • Efficient caching strategies

Lessons Learned & Future Enhancements

Key Insights

Art Sales Are Emotional Decisions

Unlike traditional e-commerce, art purchases are primarily emotional. The design must support contemplation and connection, not quick transactions.

Image Quality Can't Be Compromised

For art websites, image quality is more important than page speed. Finding the right balance required careful technical optimization.

Context Drives Confidence

Showing artwork in room settings and providing scale references significantly increased collector confidence and reduced hesitation.

Future Enhancements
